## 1. Why We Exist

At Emmanuel, we want to create "vibrant, Spirit-filled worship experiences." — Pastor Nate Ruch

The Production Team's job supports that vision by making every element of the service point people to Christ. We do that by creating a distraction-free atmosphere for people to encounter the Lord.

Creating space for God's word to move from how you hear it to how you apply it.

> "The sermon isn't over until the response has been sung."
> "Services, not shows."

## 2. Who We Are

**Dependable** — You do what you said you'd do. We deliver under tight timelines and high expectations, every week as a team.

**Approachable** — Production gets intense. But intensity never changes how we treat each other. Even in the busiest, most stressful moments, we stay patient and easy to work with.

**Flexible** — Details are bound to change, sometimes last minute. We stay flexible because we need to adapt to the moment.

## 3. How the Room Works

**Broadcast originates. Locations carry it the last mile.**

There is one production standard at Emmanuel. Every room runs the core of it. Broadcast (SLP) runs the core *plus* the roles that serve every other room — because what broadcast produces doesn't just fill the broadcast sanctuary, it feeds every location and everyone watching online.

So the team comes in two layers:

- **Core roles** — run in every room, broadcast and locations alike. The production baseline. Same standard everywhere.
- **Broadcast roles** — run only at broadcast, to produce and deliver the service to every location and online: content created once and seen everywhere.

A location isn't a lesser version of the service. It's the same service run by a leaner team — because broadcast is carrying the roles that scale across all of them.

## 4. The Roles

### Service Producer

The Service Producer owns the room. Every detail of the service — flow, timing, people, problems — runs through this seat. When leadership or a guest has a reaction to the service, good or bad, it lands here first.

The job is to lead and influence every part of the service so it serves the people in the room and points them to Christ.

At a location, the Service Producer also takes the broadcast feed to the screens.

**Before every service, four questions:**
1. Does our service fit our vision?
2. Do we have the team to pull it off?
3. Do we have all the content we need?
4. Are service element times correct?

### Timekeeper

The Timekeeper runs the clock. Every service element has an allotted time, and the Timekeeper tracks each one against the plan in real time — so the team knows when we're on, ahead, or running long.

- Have PCO logged in and running before the service starts.
- Track each element against its planned time.
- The numbers you log are the data we use to plan better — accuracy matters.

**The filter:** *"If fifty people were relying on my role to do theirs well, what's the best way to serve them?"*

### FOH Audio

FOH (Front of House) runs the mix the room hears. Creating a warm tone that is not harsh to make sure nothing about the sound pulls focus from the experience happening in the room.

- Know the console and system inside out; understand signal routing.
- Build and maintain the in-ear monitor mixes; troubleshoot personal mixers fast.
- Collaborate with the Worship Leader and MD to shape the mix.
- Worship mix: vocals peeking just above the band — even.
- Protect the room. Keep levels exciting but safe for everyone in it.

**Loudness target:** 88–94 dBA during full-energy moments.

### Lighting Operator

If words communicate meaning, lighting communicates mood. The Lighting Operator runs the looks that match each moment of the service — and makes sure the room never sits in the dark when it shouldn't.

- **Think sunsets, not lightning bolts.** Light the service, don't perform a light show.
- Make sure everyone on the platform is fully lit with key lighting at the right times.
- House lights: bright enough to read a paper Bible during the message; about half as bright during worship.
- **Haze: ever-present, never noticed.** Low and slow, like smoking ribs.
- Run the looks live and adapt when the service changes on you (extended worship, altar time, an audible).

### Graphic Operator (ProPresenter)

The Graphic Operator runs ProPresenter — lyrics, scripture, and screen content — as an extension of the worship team and the speaker. Know the songs. When a lyric is late or wrong, it pulls people straight out of worship.

- Advance lyrics and scripture in time with the platform; know where the song is going.
- Create and edit items in ProPresenter as the service requires.

### Broadcast Audio

Broadcast Audio mixes the service for our online attenders. Our online experience is our digital front door. This is the first thing people will see and hear before visiting Emmanuel in person.

### Video Director

The Video Director is responsible for making sure each visual element of service is shown in the correct place at the correct time. This includes directing camera operators to capture engaging compositions and be in place for smooth transitions. The primary goal of the Video Director is to capture the heart and vision of Emmanuel for multiple platforms which include the in-room experience, online live stream, and locations.
