## Overview

Written on behalf of Lead Pastors Dr. Nate and Jodi Ruch, signed by **Pastor JonCarlos Velez, Lead Worship Pastor.** Emmanuel has had word and worship at its core for 50+ years. The worship team's purpose is not just to sing songs but to lead the church and encourage each person to worship Jesus wholeheartedly.

> "We care about you more than what you do... it's about becoming better disciples of Christ."

## Who We Are — Identity Values

**Postured for Praise:** Public worship is an overflow of private time with Jesus. Come prepared.

**Humble:** No matter the gifts or talents, focus is always on Christ. "We are built off the sacrifices of many, not the talents of a few."

**Prepared:** "We prepare so we can play skillfully and with a joyful sound" (Psalm 33:3).

## Serving on the Team

### Preparation
- Materials (set list, notes) posted on **Planning Center** a few days before each service
- Memorize music — no music stands or sheet music on platform (distraction + obstacle to worship expression)
- Prepare your heart privately before arriving; public worship is the overflow

### Rehearsals
- Be punctual and in position before all scheduled rehearsals and service times
- Be ready to make changes; lean in to what the Lord has for the service
- Work with all music/vocal directors with a helpful and positive attitude
- Emmanuel's standard is excellence — soundcheck and rehearsal should not be where you figure out your part

## Roles

### Worship Leader (WL)
The leader who sets the spiritual and musical direction for the service. All other roles support and follow the WL.

### FLVs (Front Line Vocalists)
"Be a thermostat, not a thermometer." Sing with excellence, worship with outward expression, lead with energy.
- Support the WL: pray for the service, know where the WL is going at all times
- Show it: communicate belief through expression; be confident; don't just know the song, **show the song**

### Piano
Lead on chord changes and flow moments.
- Provide tonal rhythm
- Be intentional in quiet moments — use voicings to add interest, don't over-play
- Listen to other musicians and play together

### Keys
The energy of the sonic platform.
- Lead transitions and key changes; don't stop playing unless it's intentional
- Be available to play with the band and speaker at all times
- Follow the energy of the room

### Acoustic Guitar
Percussive leader alongside piano and drums.
- Provide tonal rhythm; be intentional with strumming patterns (mimic drums, piano, or counter-rhythms)
- Provide structure in down moments; know when picking vs. strumming

### Electric Guitar
Driving force for lead lines and supporting chord voicings.
- Carry the lead lines
- Fill the space: know your tonal placement in the mix; know when to swell and complement the dynamic
- Listen in to keys and piano

### Bass
Sonic foundation that ties rhythmic elements to the rest of the band.
- Provide movement and momentum; partner with drummer for pocket and feel
- Drive the big moments with 8th notes; drop out in quiet moments

### Drums
Dynamic energy leader and rhythmic backbone.
- Lead dynamically: follow the dynamic feel from WL and MD; track the Pastor's energy and follow momentum
- Lead rhythmically: know the feel of the song part for part

### MD (Music Director)
Bridge between the worship leader, production team, and band.
- Unify the team musically: follow the WL; communicate song form to the band; lead through transitions; know the WL hand signals and Nashville number system
- Uphold communication between production and worship: understand service flow (where worship begins, ends, what may change); provide clear direction to the service producer on changes
- Know **Ableton**: launch tracks and change clicks; cut tracks mid-song

## Hand Signals

| Signal | Gesture | Meaning |
|--------|---------|---------|
| Chorus | Cupped hand | Go to or repeat the Chorus |
| Verse | Two-finger point | Go to or repeat the Verse |
| Bridge | Flat hand/wave | Go to the Bridge |
| Tag | Index finger point | Play and/or repeat the tags |
| Dynamic Shift | Toe tap | Bring it up or bring it down |
| Ending | Fist | End the song strong |
| Instrumental | Thumbs up | Play the instrumental |
| Wait | Open palm | Bring it down and pause; listen to MD/WL |
| Cut Click | Index finger slice | Stop the click or track |

## Worship Team Ethics

### Platform Leadership
Accepting platform leadership at Emmanuel comes with a higher level of accountability in how you conduct your life. You are seen by members and outside parties as a representative of the church.

### Biblical Sexuality
Emmanuel holds the orthodox biblical view that marriage between one man and one woman is the context for sexual relationship. Leaders are called to a higher standard of accountability in relationships — no pornography, inappropriate media, premarital sexual activity (physical or digital).

### Social Media
Your online presence reflects the church even when ECC isn't mentioned. What you promote and how you influence others should be thoughtfully considered. "We have a responsibility to be a testimony in the culture."

### Alcohol Use
Leaders have a responsibility to be Christlike in all behavior.
- No alcohol on church property or at church events / Emmanuel-sponsored gatherings
- No alcohol in the company of minors you lead
- Be thoughtful about place and manner of consumption as a reflection on Christ, His church, and your testimony

### Substance Abuse
Any involvement with drugs or alcohol that affects the church, other members, or the reputation of the ministry is unacceptable. Team members struggling in this area are encouraged to reach out to their location pastor or worship leader.

### Worship Team Care
Unity is key. Healthy teams discuss conflict directly — no gossip. Build deep relationships. If facing a crisis, reach out to Location Worship Staff; they can help connect you with counselors and resources.
