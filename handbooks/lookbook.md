Emmanuel Worship's platform attire guide. Every clothing decision for platform members should serve the congregation's focus on God — not distract from it. Covers do's and don'ts, broadcast-specific color guidance, and fit rules.

---

## Core Philosophy

> "The clothes we wear need to be considered with intentionality. 1 Samuel 16:7 says '...People look at the outward appearance, but the Lord looks at the heart.' So, although God looks at the heart, people still look at the outward appearance; we should aim to dress with excellence and modesty as we represent Emmanuel. Personal style should never cross the line of distraction. Your worship expression should speak more boldly than your clothing."

---

## DO Wear

- **Earth tones**
- **Layers**
- **Solids** (add a jacket if you want)
- **Cool colors, natural tones, pastels** — keeps focus on your face, not your outfit
- **Blue** — calming; great on camera

---

## DON'T Wear

- Tight patterns or checkered shirts — conflicts with broadcast cameras (moiré effect; not applicable at non-broadcast campuses)
- T-shirts with words or large logos
- Pants with major holes or unsightly tears; no holes where skin shows above the knee (distressed denim OK)
- White jackets or high-contrast outfits — white glows on video
- **Shorts on platform** — not permitted
- Over-accessorizing
- Flip flops (not recommended)

### Colors to Avoid
- **White** — too bright on video; can glow/blow out
- **Too much black** — too dark, especially next to brighter colors; makes pale skin look paler
- **Hot, bright colors** — hot pink, orange, yellow, purple — can distract and make skin look flushed
- **Too much red** — distracting; can make face look flushed

---

## Fit Rules (Shirts)

- Not too tight
- Must not show stomach when raising your hands
- Must fully cover shoulders
- Must fully cover back when kneeling
- Always bring a backup outfit — spills happen, and uncertainty about appropriateness should be resolved before you're on stage

---

## Broadcast-Specific Notes

TV cameras can't handle high contrast between bright and dark objects, including skin and clothing.
- Pale skin: avoid black and very dark colors (look paler); pastels and earth tones work well
- Darker skin: avoid white and very light colors
- Pastels are the recommended safe zone for all skin tones on camera

---

## Accessories & Extras

- **Hats:** OK if the brim doesn't cast a shadow over the face
- Consider how every piece and every accessory will move with your body as you jump, dance, kneel, and raise your hands
